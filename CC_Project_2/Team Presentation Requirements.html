20-Minute Team Presentation Requirements

Guidelines for Presentations on Project 2 or Project 3

1. Presentation Team Guidelines

  • Full participation: Ensure all team members attend and speak.
  • Balanced speaking time: Divide approximately evenly (e.g., 3–4 minutes per member for 5 members).
  • Smooth transitions: Practice handoffs so the presentation flows naturally.
  • Clear communication: Speak clearly, avoid jargon, and briefly explain technical concepts.
  • Audience engagement: Use visuals, examples, and storytelling to maintain interest.
  • Time management: Target ~18 minutes for content plus 2 minutes for Q&A. Use timers during rehearsals.

3. Visuals & Demo Tips

  • Use clear, well-labeled screenshots, mockups, and diagrams.
  • Explicitly note simulated or conceptual elements (e.g., “Simulated Login Flow”).
  • Keep slides clean and concise—avoid dense text blocks.
  • Highlight critical user interactions and backend workflows during demos.

5. Preparation & Delivery Tips

  • Anticipate questions about your design choices, technologies, and trade-offs.
  • Practice speaker transitions and cue each other clearly.
  • Support teammates during Q&A for smooth handling of questions.
  • Prepare backup materials in case of demo or technical issues (screenshots, recordings).

6. Additional Best Practices

  • Maintain consistent visual style and branding throughout slides.
  • Use simple animations sparingly to improve clarity (e.g., stepwise reveals).
  • Include acknowledgments or references if applicable.
  • Rehearse multiple times for confidence and polish.
